Current Position and Educational Background

Claudia Bullock currently serves as a Contracting Officer's Representative for the Division of Quality Improvement (DQI), CMS Kansas City. She earned a BA in Philosophy, from the University of Maryland at College Park (UMCP), graduating with departmental honors, in 1981. Subsequently, she was trained as a Computer Programmer, graduating from Computer Learning Center (Springfield, VA) with honors in Computer Programming in 1984, and later earning a BS in Computer & Information Science, from the University of Maryland, University College (UMUC), where she graduated with honors in 1995.

Work History

After obtaining her Certificate in Computer Programming, Claudia began a career as a Computer Programmer. Prior to joining CMS, Claudia gained years of experience in this capacity working for Analytic Services (ANSER), Lerner Law Book, Co., Washington Law Book Co., and the DC Government Commission of Public Health Data Center, before finally beginning work at CMS headquarters in Baltimore, MD, in October of 1995. Claudia joined CMS (which was then called HCFA) as a Computer Specialist, participating in the primary work of the Research Support Systems Branch, which was the fulfillment of requests for Medicare and Medicaid data, in order to support research. In order to support the requests for Medicaid data she was often required to write adhoc programs to manipulate data from the Medicaid Statistical Information System (MSIS) files, or the State Medicaid Research (SMRF) files. In this regard, She was used as a programming resource for offices such as the OIG, GAO, and the HCFA Medicaid Bureau. Programs were written in an IBM Mainframe environment, using COBOL, SAS, and JCL.

Claudia transferred from the Central Office in Baltimore, to the Kansas City Regional Office in 1996, after her husband was accepted to the Osteopathich Medical School in Kansas City. Claudia continued to work as a computer programmer in Kansas City for many years, serving as a general regional resource providing support for a variety of projects. The programming necessary for completion of these projects was primarily done in SAS, both on the Mainframe and on the PC. Primary duties during this time included working with epidemiologists and statisticians on studies that utilized CMS data files, and which were published in a variety of peer-reviewed medical journals. Claudia performed the necessary programming for these studies, and also acted as co-author, writing the Methodology section, to explain what data was used, and how it was manipulated. Since coming to Kansas City in 1996, most of Claudia's career with CMS has been spent working out of the Kansas City Regional Office, however, there was a 2 year period during which she worked in the Philadelphia Regional Office as a computer programmer under a lateral transfer arrangement, which made it possible for her to stay with CMS while her husband embarked on an osteopathic internship located in Philadelphia.

Eventually Claudia transitioned from the role of Computer Programmer, to her current role as Contracting Officer's Representative (COR), at the request of her supervisor and co-workers. There was a need for additional CORs at the time due to excessive workload, and it was observed that her strong Analytic background would serve as an asset for doing this type of work.

Claudia has served as a COR for the QIO program since the 8th SOW. Currently, in the 11th SOW, her primary role is QIN-QIO COR for the Great Plains Quality Innovation Network (GPQIN), which covers the states of North Dakota, South Dakota, Nebraska, and Kansas. She also serves as a member of the DQI Internal Quality Improvement Program Team (IQIPT), and on Aim Teams 'A' and 'B'.

Personal Interests

Claudia's personal interests include the practice of nutritional excellence in order to achieve optimal health and longevity. She is a participant in the Nutritarian Women's Health Study (NWHS), which is a long term observational study on the effect of a Nutritarian diet on the occurrence, recurrence, and progression of chronic diseases (including all forms of cancer, diabetes, heart disease, and stroke).

Future Plans

Claudia is scheduled to retire from her current position on leap day (February 29th) of 2020, at which time she plans to take a big leap, by returning to a career in computer programming. Although it has been quite a few years since she has worked as a programmer, she truly misses it, as it is the type of work that she has always enjoyed the most. In order to facilitate this transition, Claudia feels blessed and honored to be a current participant in the Summer 2018 LaunchCode cohort in Kansas City. LaunchCode is providing her with exactly the right training that she needs in order to bring her skills up to date, as well as providing her with a pathway to re-enter the world of computer programming.
